# 3rd Flamerule, 1482
## Southern Ankhwood

<br>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Pushing the rest of the dim light hours to reach halfway back to town, you travel through the storm but at a slow pace as the gusts and cold rain take their toll. Fortunately, the storm eases as night begins to fall and you decide to set up your tent and have a cold meal for dinner. After eating, you make a quick but quiet patrol around the camp, ensuring you aren’t being tracked before bedding down for the night.</p>

<br>

**Q:** :: Is the night peaceful?
**A:** :: Strong Yes. No DC check vs infection for the night.

**Q:** :: Are the writings masked with the Secret Page ritual?
**A:** :: Yes, DC 20 Perception check

<br>

<p style="line-height:1.5">You sleep deeper than you intend to but wake up refreshed in the morning with no signs of yesterday’s cough. Mulling over the pages while eating your morning rations, you notice a repetitive theme on each page which piques your curiosity: teeth, fingers, blood, and spawn. You ponder in thought about why this stands out to you before you drop your half-eaten ration to the ground. *Vecna*. The name fills you with excitement and dread.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">You quickly search your pack for the vial of liquid, ensuring it hasn’t changed from when you first found it and drink the rest of your waterskin before sliding the vial carefully in and sealing the top. Confident you have another layer of protection from whatever afflicted the mad mage, you return to the pages and notice as the writing is now different. These documents are now covered in an arcane script that wasn’t there before. You carefully fold and store them safely in your pack before setting about breaking camp. The storm appears to have blown through the night but the sky remains overcast. Your pounding heart steels you against the cool damp air as you set back out with renewed vigor towards the nearby city of Ankhapur.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Making quick but stealthy progress through the forest, your eyes catch a glimpse of a raging fire up ahead. Dropping low to the ground and freezing, you calm your breath and open your senses to the world around you. Up ahead, you can hear what sounds like three distinct voices trying to be quiet but unaware of just how loud their voices are before a fourth cuts them off sharply: "I said to be quiet, he could be here soon!"</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Silence returns to the forest, and you quietly curse to yourself about the lack of wildlife about. While the Ankhwood isn’t the healthiest of forests, you should have still expected to hear some wildlife. Against your better judgement, you decide to inch closer to see what you’d possibly be dealing with and after 30 feet, spot two human shapes facing towards the fire. You’re also able to get a better look at the fire and see a raging campfire – much bigger than necessary. Assuming you might be there target, you look for the bait to see a book lying near the fire with some sort of symbol on it, one that makes your blood run cold: a hand with an eye.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Questions circle your head before you snap out of it and only one remains:</p>

{{descriptive

<p style="line-height:1.5">You opt to mull on this one while settling into a comfortable vantage point, still 30 feet away from the horribly hidden humans up ahead. Your patience rewards some knowledge as you see them continue to shift and otherwise give away their positions, noting that the two other voices unaccounted for are on the opposite side of the fire. It seems the assume the bait is important enough to guard the book over ambushing you in the wild.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Hoping you don’t regret it, you ready your shuriken and let loose at the first target, aiming a little low and </p>

\page

<p style="line-height:1.5"> hitting them square in the back, instead of in the neck. They let out a yelp of pain as their last action before your blade returns to your hand. Before the third can drop down, you loose your shuriken again, this time silently killing your target. You too quietly drop down to the forest floor and begin to slowly crawl forward to get a better aim on the remaining humans.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Watching as one of the humans crawls to flank you, you move forward and away from the fire until you get a clear shot. This time your shuriken clips a branch and flies high above your target. You see them lock eyes with you and shout "He’s over here!" Hoping you’re low enough to the ground, you recall your blade as you rely on your ears to tell you of the fourth human’s location.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Hearing a significant rustle, you chance to break eye contact to see the fourth human standing up and start running in your general direction, their eyes betraying that you remain hidden from sight.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">The human you tried to kill stands up and runs towards you, swiping down with their club as you roll out of the way. Quickly getting to your own feet, you swipe back with your shuriken, slicing deep, and they collapse to the ground. Having revealed your position, the final human spots you just to their right and turns to attack with their own club, which you easily deflect. Suddenly, a series of magic missiles appear from beside the fire as a mage finishes the incantation and they collide with you, almost knocking you off balance.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Drawing your shortsword, you deftly strike at the figure in front of you, but swing too slow and are parried by their club. Once again, another round of magic missiles flies towards you and connects, knocking the wind out of you. Unhappy with the odds, you bring your weight to bare on the next strike and knock the club out of the way and leave a deep gash in the man’s stomach. He clutches over in pain and falls to the ground screaming. *Damn you, die!* Shouts the mage as another round of magic missile collides with you. You let out a yelp as the force racks your body before steading your gaze on the mage. You charge forward, swinging your sword at the mage, knocking them off their feet. The mage stands up and slams their staff into the ground causing a burst of thunder to erupt outwards, knocking you off balance as they move away.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">You struggle to maintain focus from the aftereffects of the blast but feel your new leather armor tighten as your vision clears enough to throw your shuriken. Your training pays off as what should be a miss under normal circumstances turns into a deadly throw as your shuriken lands in the shoulder of the mage, erupting with its own thunder. Looking bloodied themselves, the mage staggers before preparing another cast of magic missiles, which again find their home in your chest. You once again purse the mage, swiping deftly with your sword, but your dazed aim is not accurate enough to connect with the mage.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">The mage steps back, chanting a different spell as lightning shoots out from his staff towards you, but arcs wildly. The mage curses as you step forward, swinging again with a sly flourish as your blade subtly glows and you cut the source of much pain down unceremoniously. Seeing no other threat nearby, you opt to bandage yourself before inspecting the book. Your patience rewards you, as you notice a decently concealed rope leading away from the book into the nearby brush. Standing back, you expertly throw your shuriken and cut the rope, springing the trap harmlessly. Confident in your safety, you walk over to the book lying on the ground and mutter in disgust as you confirm the illustration to be that of Vecna’s followers.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Knowing the arcane is not where your strengths lie, you tie the book closed with some of the rope and add it to your pack. One cultist in an abandoned monastery is one thing, but a group using the perfect bait to prevent you from returning to town is another. You kick dirt on the fire and quickly search the other cultists and confirm your suspicion by finding tattoos of a hand and eye hidden away on their waists. Searching the mage, you find an amulet of protection, which you quickly put on and proceed to hide the bodies in the brush.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Instead of heading directly south to the ruins of Dalelost, you opt to skirt the edge of the forest to the west and follow the river south to the road. Fortunately, the rest of the journey is uneventful as you make quick work of the distance to the bridge as the sight of the Arnrock and Lake of Steam come into view.</p>

A couple blocks in, you reach a market square where you hang a right and proceed to what can only be described as the adventurer’s district: plenty of taverns, inns, and guilds constantly looking for fresh blood. Seven more blocks north and you finally find yourself outside the Rusty Chain, the local home of the Delver's of Ioun|delver's. You push open the thick wooden door to a well-lit tavern and see Palmer behind the counter, the half-elf proprietor.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Walking up to the bar, you cordially greet Palmer and order the daily special with a gross tip – tossing 5 gold onto the counter. “That good or that bad?” Palmer asks inquisitively as she moves towards the kitchen and relays your order.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">“A bit of both really,” you wince as you sit down, the wounds still a bit fresh and your body aching from the fast journey. “Let’s just say I need to visit the healers and the library before I can truly relax.”</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">“A tip that big means you must’ve found something though?” Palmer asks with a smile as she leans on the counter. “Come on, I know you delver’s are all about finding information, that’s why I welcome you here. <p style="line-height:1.5">“After business is over,” you drop five more gold on the counter, “and save me a good room at least?” A call comes from the kitchen and Palmer scoops up the coin, heading off to get your meal. You set your pack down, wincing again from your two scuffles, thankful a third didn’t occur. Palmer knew just what strings to play, maybe it was aided by her half-elven charm, or the years spent working at the Chain. *A fair exchange of information*, that phrase cut a little deeper as you thought about the contents of your pack. * Vecna, the keeper of secrets and his cult was close.* Palmer returns shortly after, carrying a steaming hot stew and a mug of cold ale, setting them down in front of you. She steps away to grab a key before returning, “Room 4, middle on the left, just how you like it.”</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">You savor the stew as much as you can between big bites, a hot meal does much to heal the spirit after being out in the wilds, and the cool ale helps beat back the heat of the summer day. Being so close to the lake, the humidity made the trek almost unbearable in the new dark leathers. You take a moment to loosen the straps, letting your skin breathe before finishing your ale and grabbing your pack. You nod to Palmer and set back out into Ankhapur, heading towards the temples.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">Navigating the streets, you find yourself lost in</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">thought about the previous day’s events and your mind wanders to the book you refused to open.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">You wonder to yourself as you cut through another market square and arrive at the southeastern end of the city. On a small hill you can see the harbor to the west and the bulk of the city to the north. Looking around, you spy the temple of Pelor just ahead, on the right.</p>

<p style="line-height:1.5">The orange symbol of the sun seems to glow in the hot summer light as you approach the stone building. The doors open and a few people leave as you enter. The inside is well lit, using numerous windows to let the summer in, but the heat inside is more comforting than outside, and you find yourself beginning to relax a bit. Finding a priest, you offer 10 gold pieces in exchange for some healing words to soothe your soul and body. You’re taken to one of the many rooms in the back where you shed your armor and the priest sighs and gives you a look after noting the number of crude bandages covering your body. “Goblins,” you say carefully, “almost more than I could handle.”</p>
