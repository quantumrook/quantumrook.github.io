# 2nd Flamerule, 1482

> Diseased, Blood, Temporary, Whoosh
> In the diseased thicket of the [[Ankhwood]], an old monastery stands amongst a raging storm. Blood marks the stone entrance.

The wind is currently blowing strongly, sending raindrops cutting about like tiny razors, but you can maintain solid footing with minor effort.

## The Diseased Monastery: Room 1

> - Room 1: Deadly Encounter
> - 4 goblin cutters, 1 goblin sharpshooter
> Q: Are the goblins making noise?
> A: Yes, they are complaining loudly (in goblin) about a campfire.

Approaching the overgrowth on the path, you can hear the sounds of goblins that seem to be arguing around what appears to be a dwindling campfire. 

> Stealth check: 18
> Pass

Instinctively, your roguish instincts took over as you approached the narrowing path, and you began to step lighter and avoided foliage while pressing forward. The storm also offers another layer of concealment, but experience has taught you well.

> Q: Is the growth thin enough to throw something through?
> A: No, while you can make out the shapes of goblins and the flicker of flames beyond, there's not a clear enough path that you think you could make a successful throw through.

> Q: Can I squeeze through (vs force my way through)?
> Maybe: Use a skill challenge, DC 15

		Complete Success: Gain a surprise round
		Success: You make it through, start initiative normally
		Complete Failure: You start in difficult terrian (in the thicket) and goblins gain a surprise round

		Acrobatics: 19
		Stealth: 10
		Nature: 12

After taking a moment to gauge the feasibility of getting a sneak attack off from behind the thicket, you decide its better to be on the other side before engaging. As a rogue, you’re used to navigating difficult spaces, and so contorting your body barely requires an effort from you. However, you misjudged the health of some branches and cause a series of loud cracking noises as you exit the thicket – loud enough that the goblins nearby turn and yelp.

The goblin sharpshooter quickly readies his crossbow and fires a bolt at you, grazing your skin but still drawing blood. Riding the momentum of the impact, you spin around and loose your shuriken at the closest goblin, hitting him square in the chest and killing him as he stumbles back into the campfire. Being well practiced in this style of combat, your hand grabs the shuriken as it teleports back to you as you finish your spin. Letting loose again, the shuriken flies towards the goblin next to the sharpshooter and lands in his neck. The goblin tries to grab the shuriken in surprise but finds nothing as it teleports back to your hand again. The other two remaining goblins brandish their short-swords and charge forward swiping at you. The first swipe misses but the second catches you mid-dodge, cutting into your side.

The sharpshooter starts to tremble as he reloads his crossbow, waiting for a moment of distraction to fire. Seeing you clutch your side, he lets loose another bolt, this time finding purchase in your leather armor. Quickly regaining your composure, you tumble away from the two cutters and towards the sharpshooter. Finishing your roll with a small leap, you bring your shortsword down at the sharpshooter, cutting into his arm. The two cutters, confused for a moment, quickly run to the aid of their comrade and swipe at your back, both finding purchase.

The storm swells, as if in tune with the combat, and a gust of wind rips through the battlefield. Only the sharpshooter loses their footing, falling to the ground. Realizing how vulnerable they are, they quickly swipe while trying to stand up at you and miss. Your grip your duelist shortsword with finesse and slash at the sharpshooter’s face. Cutting into their forehead, blood rushes down into their eyes, blinding them. The two cutters stumble before regaining their footing and slash again, cutting deep into the leather armor.

The sharpshooter swings wildly in a panic at you, missing wide with his shortsword as you stab deftly into his chest, killing him.

		Morale Check:
		- Cutter 3 Fails
		- Cutter 2 Passes

One of the cutters breaks formation and begins to run towards the stone door at the eastern edge of the clearing. The other swipes once again at you, seeing your death as their only escape, and again finds purchase. The storm declines as you turn around to face the remaining two goblins.

		Morale Check:
		- Cutter 3 Fails
		- Cutter 2 Passes

> Q: Is the stone door locked?
> A: No

Not wanting to risk more company joining the fray, you shift to the side away from the nearest goblin and close the distance and impale the goblin at the door from behind.

		Morale Check:
		- Cutter 2 Passes

The sole remaining goblin steel’s his resolve and charges after you, swinging madly and catches you off guard leaving a deep cut into your legs. You struggle to keep standing as the wind picks back up, but manage to dig deep enough to turn and face your final foe. With a deft strike, you hope to fell your opponent and manage to catch his sword with yours, knocking it away before stabbing him in the chest.

Unsure of what lies inside, you opt to recover your strength and tend to your wounds at the side of the fire. Fortunately, no cut was too deep, but you did have to crudely sow your leather armor back into a manageable piece. Sitting opposite the door, you warm your rations with the fire, trying to stave off the storm as you recover.

		Endurance Check:
		Pass

> Q: Does the stone door open as a result of the fight?
> A: No

## The Diseased Monastery: Room 2

> - Room 2: The Foray
> - Dungeon Hazard: Fungus

> Q: Is the fungus airborne?
> A: Yes

> Q: Is there notable evidence of fungus in the room?
> A: No, nothing immedaitely apparent

> Q: Are there monsters in the room?
> A: Yes, medium difficulty
>  - 4 goblin warriors, 1 goblin blackblade, 4 cutters

> Q: Does the fungus do damage every round?
> A: No

> Q: Are the monsters weakened from the fungus? (Highly Likely)
> A: Somewhat: They start with -10 HP. All cutters are dead on the floor.

After recovering as much as you dare in the storm near the stone entrance to the old monastery, you decide to test the door. Standing near and observing it, you don’t see any traps or any locking mechanisms on this side, and pressing your ear to the crack, you hear more goblins inside. Realizing that the storm will ruin any chance of sneakily opening the door, you ready your shuriken in one hand and a sun rod in the other. 

> Q: Are the goblins aware of your presence?
> A: Yes

> Q: Are they ready?
> A: Yes

Kicking the door open, you toss the sun rod inside and to your horror see two goblin warriors with javelins aimed at you. The first javelin sails by your head outside into the storm and the second one clangs against the other half of the door, falling to the ground. Unseen, however, is a Blackblade who emerges from aside the door and swipes at you with their sword, digging into the leather on your arm.

Quickly grabbing your sword, you swipe in the same motion at the Blackblade preparing a riposte. The initial strike hits and you jump backwards into the storm, as another javelin flies through the doorway where you just were. Now with both warriors out of view, you hear a frustrated goblin scream over the storm and suddenly run into the portion of the room you can still see through the doorway. He throws another javelin, but awkwardly hitting you. While the tip doesn’t find purchase the force of the impact makes you stumble briefly. The blackblade then steps into the doorway and swipes at you but you interrupt with your prepared riposte – cutting into his chest with ease. He slumps down to the ground lifelessly.

		Morale Check:
		- Warrior 1 & 2 Pass

		Fungus Check:
		- Warrior 1 crit fails. Will take additional 3 damage at start of next turn.
		- Warrior 2 fails

The furthest warrior readies another spear and misses. You step back into the doorway and jab at the closest goblin with your sword, landing a devastating strike. He attempts to respond but begins coughing violently before slumping to the floor dead. The second warrior begins to panic but decides to throw one more javelin before turning tail and running. He lets his panic override his training and fails to ensure his feet are planted properly before letting go, causing the javelin to crash into the ceiling. He runs towards the stairs, but your shuriken finds purchase between his armor at his neck, and he skids to a halt at the top of the staircase.
Taking a moment to look around, you see three doorways in the room: a collapsed staircase to the north, a smashed wooden door directly east, and an intact staircase to the south. You also notice 4 additional goblin corpses spread out across the room. While they did not die in the recent fight with you, their cause of death seems to be difficult to determine.

		Endurance Check vs Fungus:
		Pass

		Fungal Disease Effects:
		- DC 15 Fort Save to avoid infection
		- Once failed, move at half speed
		- Infected creature grants combat advantage
		- DC 12 Endurance check to avoid taking 5 ongoing damage

Moving into the backroom, the warm moldy air hits your nostrils quite quickly, but you instinctively cover your mouth before breathing. The fungal mage has been sustaining invisibility since the goblins entered the room earlier. Suddenly, magic missiles fly out from the back corner and strike you in the chest. The air shimmers as the shape of a man appears, glowing a dim purple light emits from the left side of its upper body. A second set of missiles comes flying from the mage, but you manage to duck before they collide. You quickly swipe with your sword setting up a riposte and connect with the crystal growth in the mage’s arm.

Reeling from the hit, the mage shifts away and releases another set of missiles which hit you again in the chest. Charging forward, you swipe at them with a sly flourish, your blade cutting deep. The mage reaches out and grabs you and breathes a wave of purple mist into your face. He then pushes you before shifting away again. You suffer a violent coughing fit, letting the mage get some distance between you before attempting to give chase. With the mage’s back to you, your blade finds purchase in their back, and they collapse to the ground as you withdraw your blade.

You manage to stifle a cough, while taking stock of the old bathroom. The mage appeared to be guarding some alchemy supplies and searching the body reveals a crystalline growth stemming from their mouth, down their neck, and up to their eyes. The face no longer resembles that of a human but of some horror used to keep children from playing in the woods after sunset. The skin is already cold to the touch, implying this growth happened after they had passed. You make a note about being coughed on and quicken your search of the area. The robes contain what appear to be mad scribbles and you pocket them for later. You also find one vial of liquid that glows the same color as the growth on their face and gingerly store it in your pack.

Returning to the foray with the goblins, you again cough violently as you search the bodies and find a finely tailored leather pack on one of the goblins. The Blackblade’s armor appears to still be intact compared to yours and of rough same size. You deduce that it was looted off some other poor adventurer and quickly swap your current jacket for the goblin’s and tuck your old magicked leathers away. Coughing again, you resign to returning to explore the remaining path at a later date, else you suffer the same fate of the mage, and brace yourself as you return to the raging storm outside. The fresh cool air eases the weight on your lungs and after a few minutes you feel your breath and strength return.
