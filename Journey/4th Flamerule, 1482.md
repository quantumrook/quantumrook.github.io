# 4th Flamerule, 1482
## [[Ankhapur]], Proper

Meeting early in the morning, downstairs before the sun rises, you introduce your new companion to [[Palmer]]. With no other patrons awake this early, Palmer shares old tales of your work for the [[Delver's of Ioun|Delver's]] and your bond deepens with Leshanna as she sees the truth in your tales and your devotion to [[Ioun]]. You place ten more gold pieces on the counter, “Well, with that I think we best get going. Palmer, save my room if you can, I expect to be back within the tenday.”

 Palmer scoops up the coin and nods. [[Leshanna]] nods to Palmer before standing up, stretching, and grabbing her pack. You quickly do the same, “I think a few merchant friends might be open on the way out of the town. Shall we retrace my steps and risk unwanted company or take the longer but safer new route?” You ask Leshanna as you both step outside into the surprisingly cool and humid summer air. She ponders for a bit before looking at you,

 “If they would challenge us, I would rather thin their numbers now,” she replies. You nod and take the lead heading towards the market square near the southeastern gate.

 Approaching the square as the sun peaks above the city walls, you see a number of merchants still setting up their stalls. Looking about, you spot [[Pepper]] in his favorite spot, already set up. With the square being relatively empty, its not long before he sees you and your companion crossing it before he calls out to you. You introduce Leshanna to Pepper and purchase two waterskins and give a quick look over Leshanna’s gear. “I actually think you’re pretty well covered as far as gear goes, unless you need any spell components?”

 “I stocked up before meeting with [[Denal]].”

 “Then we best be off. Thanks again Pepper!” You say as you turn to leave the city.

 ## Southeastern Gate, [[Ankhapur]]
 
 > Q: Is there an encounter at the gate/in the city? E.g. the cultists are feeling brazen enough in the early morning hours?
 > A: Yes. Keywords: Hairpin, Protection, Advance
 > _You see workers loading a huge shipment into carts. You wonder what this shipment is, where it might be going, who it's for, and whether they have protection on the journey._

As you step out past the mostly refurbished guard post, you notice a group of workers loading an unusually large shipment of crates back onto carts. Your curiosity peaks as you note a lack of sellswords present and wonder if they have enough protection to reach their destination.

> Q: Are the workers hostile?
> A: Yes

> Q: Does it feel like an ambush?
> A: Maybe. DC 13 Insight check.

> Q: Are they cultists?
> A: Yes

You look to Leshanna, “that’s a rather large shipment for no protection. Even if they’re not expecting trouble on the road...”

“You seek to offer protection or something else?”

		Insight Check:
		29, Pass

> _Something is definitely off about the workers. Maybe their mannerisms or how they're loading the crates feels wrong, but this reads as more than just a simple naive trade convoy._

		Encounter Diffculty: Medium
		2 Human Bandits, 7 Human Rabble


“I’m not sure, something feels off.” Your voice lowers to a whisper. “See how they’re loading those crates? If I didn’t know better, I’d say they were empty. But this close to the gates? Who would be so brazen or stupid?”

Leshanna, picking up on your unease grips her staff tighter and whispers back, “Your _friends_?” Scanning the area around, it then occurs to you that there is a distinct lack of number of guards at the gate.

“Ambush.” You say dryly, drawing your sword.

“Get them!” A cry calls out in response and the _workers_ drop the hollow crates, reaching for weapons.

Moving quickly, you pull your shuriken from your belt and spin, letting it fly towards the nearest attacker, killing him instantly. Leshanna raises her staff and charges forward. She brings her staff down with a loud crack and her target slumps down lifelessly onto the pile of crates nearby. One of the rabble charges forward and catches Leshanna unaware with a swing of his club. Most of the cultists swarm towards Leshanna with a few heading towards [[Quinn]] in the back.

Opting to close the distance himself, Quinn meets one of the tougher looking cultists head on, setting up a riposte. Unfazed by the number of cultists around her, Leshanna raises her staff and begins an incantation. Seeing an opportunity to attack, the nearby cultists swing their clubs and miss. Lightning arcs down from the sky, centered on the group of cultists, killing 3 of the 4. The remaining cultists continue to swarm Leshanna, one swiping with a mace, and succeeds in dazing her. As the mace connects, Leshanna’s armor glows a bright blue before sending out a shockwave of radiant light, knocking the cultist back 10 feet. The other cultist rushes forward but trips over his own feet, faceplanting on the ground next to Leshanna.

The cultist next to Quinn lifts his mace, but Quinn, anticipating this, strikes first, stabbing the cultist in the gut. However, the mace still comes down hard on Quinn, dazing him as well. Siezing the chance, one of the few remaining underlings strikes Quinn with his club, knocking Quinn back. Still reeling from the dazing hit, Quinn swipes with his sword towards the underling, hoping to even the odds and succeeds in deftly cutting them down. Leshanna follows suit with another crack of her staff, leaving only the two henchmen standing.

Seeing Quinn otherwise engaged, the furthest henchman throws a dagger at him but aims too high and misses. Quinn ducks out of the way as the other henchmen makes another swipe at him with the mace. Seeing that he is now flanked, Quinn tumbles to the side and strikes with a sly flourish, hoping to end this quickly. Quinn’s blade again finds purchase in the gut of the henchman as Leshanna’s staff begins to glow with radiant light again. Avenging light shoots forth, striking the bloodied henchman, and he collapses to the ground amidst the radiant flames.

		Morale Check:
		Bandit Fails.
		
> Q: Does he flee or surrender?
> A: Attempts to flee, most likely will resist surrender

Seeing everyone else fall, the last henchman drops his mace and runs for it. Quinn runs after him, swiping his sword and cutting deeply into the henchman’s legs. Leshanna bull rushes the henchman, knocking him prone. “Surrender or die.” She says coldly between breaths.

		Skill check for Surrender:
			- 3 Success: Surrender
			- 3 Failures: Flees
		Diplomacy  vs Will,     DC 12: Fail
		Acrobatics vs Fort,     DC 12: Pass
		Insight    vs Will,     DC 12: Fail
		Endurance  vs Fort,     DC 12: Pass
		Bluff	   vs Insight,  DC 11: Pass
		
The henchman tries to get up and run but Quinn swipes his legs out from underneath him, knocking him prone again with a loud thud. “Surely you value your life over whatever this is?” Leshanna says again coldly and bewildered at the man’s struggle. Quinn, seeing the man about to run again, jumps on top of him fighting to keep him down. Leshanna, seeing her previous attempts fail, switches to bluffing, “The guards are coming down from the north path. I’m sure you’d rather talk with us than them, they’ll definitely want to keep you alive.”

The man stops struggling long enough for Quinn to roll him over and bind his legs. “Okay okay. Just make it quick. What do you want to know?” A look of fear covers the man’s face as he struggles to look north to see the guards that aren’t there.

“Who sent you?”

“The blood.”

“Why?”

“You took something you weren’t supposed to.”

“What? I take a lot of things.”

“I don’t know, that’s the truth. Ow!” The man exclaims as Quinn puts pressure on his bleeding leg. “What do you want?! We keep secrets- Argh! Even from each other!”

“We don’t have much time Quinn.” Leshanna says, looking back to the south, seeing 5 guards approaching from the city with weapons drawn.

“Check the barrel in the second cart. Underneath the pitons.”

“How do we know you haven’t lied to us, Finger?”

“You don’t,” he replies as he sits up into the blade at his neck, cutting his own life short as the guards arrive.

“Drop your weapons!” The guards shout as they fan out around Quinn and Leshanna. She turns complies, letting her staff fall to the ground. Quinn releases his sword and holds his hands up as he rolls off the corpse, standing up.

> Q: Is [[Cirog]] among the guards?
> A: Yes

“Quinn! What the hell happened!” [[Cirog]] says, stepping forward and lowering his sword.

“[[Cirog]]! Where were you all?!” Quinn asks back angrily.

“My question first.” [[Cirog]] states coldly, slightly raising his weapon.

Slightly irritated, Leshanna cuts in, “We were ambushed by these cultists,” and she gestures to the corpses laying about the field, lastly pointing to the final one. “He killed himself rather than be captured.”

“Cultists? Here?” [[Cirog]] asks, confused, as he sheathes his weapon and signals to the other guards. “Check the rest.”

“Truth be told, we expected to run into company on the road, but not right outside the gate.” Quinn bends down, grabbing his sword and uses the corpse to wipe it clean before tucking it away. “Why was there no one at the guard post?”

“We had a frantic report of a beast that was loose in one of the market squares and went to check, but there still should’ve been a few inside the post…” [[Cirog]] frowns and looks to the tower. “Don’t worry, I’ll get answers to this. You are _sure_ that these were cultists?”

Quinn looks down and searches the body, finding a hairpin of the symbol he has grown all too familiar with. Holding up the hand with an eye in the palm to [[Cirog]], he says, “Unfortunately. [[Vecna]]’s followers. I fear the reason why they’d feel so emboldened to risk an ambush outside a city like Ankhapur.” [[Cirog]] takes the hairpin into his hand. “We were about to depart on a mission concerning them nearby in the Ankhwood.”

[[Cirog]] looks up from the hairpin, “Then you should make preparations to leave. I’ll brief the guard to be more watchful in the meantime.”

“We don’t know if what he said before he died was true, but he told us to check under the pitons in the barrel of the second cart. I recommend caution, while this was an ambush, it felt a bit too easy if they wanted us dead. No magic users and simple weapons.” Quinn says, dusting himself off.

“Very well, we’ll handle things here. May [[Pelor]]’s hands guide you.” [[Cirog]] says looking between the Eladrin and human.

“And [[Ioun]]’s eyes watch your step.” Quinn says a bit nervously.

## Northeast of [[Ankhapur]]

> Q: Is there an encounter on the way to the forest?
> A: No

> Is the trip effected by the weather?
> A: Yes. Strong winds.

The cool morning gives way to strong winds blowing in from the west, carrying more humidity from the lake deeper inland. While the moving air is preferable to a hot stale summer day, the windstorm kicks up dirt forcing the two to protect their faces and ensure steady footing as they cross the grasslands towards the forest. As a result, it takes the two most of the day to reach the cover of the forest, with only a few hours left of daylight.

## Southern [[Ankhwood]]

> Q: Is there an encounter in the forest?
> A: Yes

> Q: Is it more cultists?
> A: No

> Q: Does it happen immediately?
> A: No

Upon entering the edge of the forest, Quinn raises a hand and whispers to Leshanna, “We should take care. Last time the trap was obvious, this time we might not be so lucky.” Leshanna nods and the two progress into the thicket at a slower pace, careful to avoid making too much noise.

		Group Stealth Check:
			Q: 16, L: 9
			Group mod = +2
		Group's passive stealth is 15 (Lowest + Mod)

After another hour and a half of travel, Quinn signals to stop to find a place to make camp. The two drop their packs and spread out, searching the surrounding area for anything of concern. A few steps away, Leshanna freezes as she notices the lack of sounds of nature in surrounding her. Suddenly, a crow dips below the canopy and drops a scroll at her feet. Cautiously, she picks it up, scanning it for any arcane warding and finding none, she confidently breaks the seal and reads the [[Message from Dalelost|message]] within:

_Adventurer! Heed my call! I am in need of your services. Head east towards Dalelost and search for a sigil marking a door. That is where I reside. I have a proposition of great interest for you._

Leshanna crouches down and continues to observe the surroundings until she sees Quinn moving quietly toward her. In a low voice, she shows him the scroll and fills him in, noting that the sounds of nature have returned in the crow’s absence.

“We can swing by [[Dalelost]] on the way back,” Quinn says hesitantly, “I’m loathe to delay the monastery at this time; lest we miss something the cultists may be trying to hide. I’m not sure how I feel about being tracked by this other person though… I thought we would’ve avoided the eyes of a familiar… If we make a fire tonight, we should be extra cautious.” Leshanna nods and the two return to their packs, quietly unpacking their tents and covering them with brush. As the sun begins to set, Quinn takes a piton from his pack and begins to dig two holes in the ground, connecting them at the bottom. Leshanna returns with some mostly dry brush and Quinn packs it into one of the holes before lighting it with his flint.

“I only need to rest for four hours,” Leshanna says, “Would you like first watch or second?”

Quinn thinks a moment before responding, “I’ll take second, I’m a bit slow to wake up in the mornings.”

 		Watch Check: 15, encounter happens
		Antagonist Perception: 13 vs 15; Party is stealthed.
		Watch Perception:      15 vs 11; Watch sees encounter.

> Q: Is it a hostile encounter?
> A: No

Quinn puts out the fire and crawls into his tent, promptly falling quietly asleep. Leshanna sits still near the camp, slowly watching the surroundings and listening for activity. Towards the end of her watch, she hears a twig snap far in the distance and looks over to see [[Rangers in Ankhwood|2 humanoid figures]] trying to carefully move through the forest, heading southward. After a few moments, she notes that they seem to be obvious to her or the campsite and debates waking Quinn. She opts to wait longer, watching as they continue to move before crouching down for some time, and then moving again. As her watch ends, she notes that they have disappeared from sight and continued in their southern direction without hesitation.

She gently wakes Quinn and quietly fills him in before heading to her own tent to rest. Quinn takes up a southern facing position for the first half of his watch.

		Watch Check: 5, No encounter
		
Quietly moving about for the last hour until the sun rises, he notices no sign of the humanoid figures or anything else before he wakes up Leshanna. The two quietly eat their rations and break camp before heading north, deeper into the [[Ankhwood]].
